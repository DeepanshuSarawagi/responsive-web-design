Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile man of India

Below is a timeline of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Early Life: Avul Pakir Jainulabdin Abdul Kalam born on October 15th, 1931 in a pilgrimage center of Rameshwaram, Pamban Island, Tamilnadu. His father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother Ashiamma was a housewife. His father owned a ferry that took Hindu pilgrims back and forth between Rameswaram and the now uninhabited Dhanushkodi. Kalam was the youngest of four brothers and one sister in his family.

Education: Dr. Kalam was a bright student according to his teachers. He completed his primary education from Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram. And went on to graduate from Saint Joseph`s college, Thiruchirappalli. He then moved to Madras Institue of technology to study Arerospace Engineerin and went becoming Scientist at DRDO.

Career: After graduating from MIT in Arerospace Engineering, Dr. Kalam joined as scientist at DRDO. He started his career by designing a small hovercraft, but remained unconvinced by his choice of a job at DRDO. Kalam was also part of the INCOSPAR committee working under Vikram Sarabhai, the renowned space scientist. In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980; Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965. In 1969, Kalam received the government's approval expanded the programme to include more engineers. DR. Kalam developed several projects during this tenure as scientist at ISRO which earned him the title The Missile Man of India.

Presidency: After his sucessful tenure at DRDO and ISRO, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his term as president, he was affectionately known as the People's President, saying that signing the Office of Profit Bill was the toughest decision he had taken during his tenure. At the end of his term, on 20 June 2007, Kalam expressed his willingness to consider a second term in office provided there was certainty about his victory in the 2007 presidential election. However, two days later, he decided not to contest the Presidential election again stating that he wanted to avoid involving Rashtrapati Bhavan from any political processes. Many, many citizens have also expressed the same wish. It only reflects their love and affection for me and the aspiration of the people. I am really overwhelmed by this support. This being their wish, I respect it. I want to thank them for the trust they have in me.
